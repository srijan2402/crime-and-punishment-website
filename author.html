Fyodor Dostoevsky

Dostoevsky was a 19th-century Russian novelist, known for his deep exploration of psychology and morality. He was born in 1821 in Moscow and became one of the greatest literary figures in world literature.

His other famous works include The Brothers Karamazov, Notes from Underground, and The Idiot.
