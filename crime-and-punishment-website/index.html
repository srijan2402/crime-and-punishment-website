Introduction

Crime and Punishment is a psychological novel by Russian author Fyodor Dostoevsky, published in 1866. It follows the mental and moral dilemmas of Rodion Raskolnikov, a poor ex-student who believes he can commit murder for a greater good.

The novel explores themes of guilt, redemption, and justice in the face of poverty and philosophical reasoning.
